# There’s Plenty of Room at the Top | by Shaurya | Medium

We keep hearing about how humans have this thing called potential and that they should work towards maximizing it. But just how much potential is there and what does it mean to maximize it and what is this potential thing anyway? Let us resort to biology.

Your genes dictate how your life plays out in any given environment. If we hold DNA constant throughout a human’s lifespan (sorry epigenetics), we get a fixed upper limit to what a human can become measured against an arbitrary number of metrics. We are going to call this upper limit the _genetic constraint_. You can be below or on your genetic constraint but not above it. In biological terms, you cannot have phenotypic expressions that cannot be mapped to your genotype. This gives us almost a production possibility curve for a human being. A _potential accessibility curve_ if you will.

![](https://proxy-prod.omnivore-image-cache.app/700x389,sb2RSEfqPhAvM4BTIuWaqWmoVoWoanre43S5Sk7mdKK8/https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*GcR49PCwnkKbpv5M_SmDXQ.png)

Since every human has a different DNA sequence, there is a unique curve for each human. Some people obviously have a higher curve than others. How high you are inside your curve defines your potential and a point on the curve is your highest potential. How bleak and dismal! You are stuck with your curve which may be mediocre at best.

![](https://proxy-prod.omnivore-image-cache.app/700x389,sCWCnyNZaGmhu0MEpZDz8wTL7StoW16LAZiNqBYDFDuo/https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/1*4OW3OWZ5o14nPWZoC0_MKw.png)

But wait a second, where exactly do humans normally function inside their curves? Since we know that not all genes are expressed at any given time, your genes contain different versions of you with varying degrees of potential that are accessed at any given time. Some of these versions are more easily accessible than others. The ease of accessibility goes down as you go higher up in the curve. This means that it is highly probable that you, like everyone else, are currently functioning way below your genetic constraint. Trying to maximize your potential and worrying about your constraint, then, is almost like trying to build a really fast aircraft and worrying about the speed of light.

Your highest genetic expression might not be humanity’s all-time high but it may be higher than anything any human has ever expressed.
