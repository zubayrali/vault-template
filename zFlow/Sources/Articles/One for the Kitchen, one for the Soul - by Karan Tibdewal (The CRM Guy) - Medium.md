# One for the Kitchen, one for the Soul | by Karan Tibdewal (The CRM Guy) | Medium

## The importance of balancing the act

“But I wish I was doing something worthwhile, something that had an impact” I exclaimed. My now girlfriend, then a very interesting and smart woman very calmly explained her views on how work life could be balanced — and although one of the most simple advice changed my perception to a very large degree. I was sold on the idea. She told me “**Work needs to be split two ways. There needs to be one for the kitchen and one for the soul — and remember both are equally important.**”

## What it means?

The meaning is quite clear — you have to balance worldly perception of work and indulge sometimes in work that might not satisfy your soul but provides you and your family with necessities to satisfy your basic needs and enjoy guilty pleasures of life. However, your soul needs its satisfaction as well. I have seen a lot of people who, in an ideal world would like to be a teacher, or work as a freelancer, write to create impact, spread stories, research even however something common holds them down. More often than not its the income and the pressure of making ONE choice. All of these people (including past me) make a very dangerous assumption here. And that is, we need and can have only ONE thing.

## How can you satisfy your Soul?

==Similar to providing basic necessities of life through your “Kitchen” work, soul has its basic necessities as well. It requires satisfaction, feeling of importance and impact. Although once you have got your kitchen figured out, up and running you can now dive into your soul work. Once your kitchen part is fixed, your soul gets light.The best thing is, that it requires no additional pressure from the society (since kitchen is taking care of that), but only commitment. The ONLY thing it requires is your commitment to actually doing the work that is not rewarding in the financial sense but fulfils you, inspires you and motivates you — makes your curious to know more. The soul work provides you with a life outside work and a life that you enjoy. However, mind you — If the kitchen half was not taken care of, this life would not be easy if not as rewarding.==

## The most important thing to understand — Both are equally necessary and essential

Similar to the Japanese concept of Yin and Yang, doing only one kind of work is incomplete. Both Kitchen and Soul work is required for you to remove the noise that nudges you to constantly look for impact, look for a higher paying jobs. Usually when we are doing only the Kitchen work, and at times when we don’t find it rewarding enough or fulfilling enough, we start blaming others and looking for more money as a way to compensate for all the soul work we ought to be doing. The most essential take away is that you need to treat both with equal dignity and respect to gain the most out of each and make for a very fulfilling and inspiring daily life.
