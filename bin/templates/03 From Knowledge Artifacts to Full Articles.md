## 1. Review and select Knowledge Artifacts
- [ ] Use AI to find and determine relevant and interconnected KAs  (optional)

## 2. Group related KAs
- [ ] Use this template to organize the interconnected KAs into potential sections under this Header
- [ ] Identify any gaps in information that might need additional research 

## 3. Create an outline
- [ ] Based on your grouped KAs, draft a detailed outline by using the Templates
- [ ] Include main points, subpoints, and potential examples, etc.
## 4. Draft the article
This is where you flesh out the outline into full sentences and paragraphs. You're not just listing ideas anymore, but fully expressing them. In this step:
- Turn outline points into full paragraphs
- Expand on your ideas with explanations and examples
- Write a full introduction and conclusion
- Don't worry about perfection; focus on getting your ideas down
  
- [ ] Start with a rough draft, focusing on getting ideas down
- [ ] Use the Hemingway App to check for clarity and conciseness (optional)
- [ ] Incorporate storytelling elements and transitions between the main notes

## 5. Edit and refine
- [ ] Use Grammarly or ProWritingAid for initial edits (optional)
- [ ] Read aloud to check flow and identify awkward phrasing
- [ ] Ensure you're addressing your target audience (as per your audience research)

## 6. Prepare for Substack
- [ ] Export your draft from Obsidian to Substack
- [ ] Do a final readthrough, focusing on how it will appear to your readers
- [ ] Prepare any necessary HTML formatting for Substack (optional)

## 7. Final polish on Substack
- [ ] Use Substack's preview feature to check layout and formatting
- [ ] Make any final adjustments needed for the platform

## 8. SEO (optional but highly recommended) and distribution prep
- [ ] Craft a compelling title and meta description
      A good headline should include the following four Ps:
	- Problem: What problem does the article solve for the reader?
	- Person: Who is the target audience?
	- Product: What type of content is the article (e.g. tips, advice, story)?
	- Promise: What benefit will the reader get from reading the article?
	  You should use strong verbs and active voice in your headlines. This will make the headlines more engaging and attention-grabbing.
- [ ] Choose relevant tags for your article
- [ ] Prepare social media snippets for promotion
